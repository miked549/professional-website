Common Engineering Challenges in the Manufacturing Industry

XYZ Plastics has helped many manufacturers overcome complex engineering problems with innovative solutions. Our engineers consult with your in-house teams to uncover process inefficiencies and opportunities for improvement. Some of the factors we examine include:

To prevent production run costs from spiraling out of control, it’s essential to minimize manufacturing costs prior to scaling. Some of the most common causes of high production costs include process bottlenecks, equipment downtime, expensive replacement parts, high energy costs, unreliable suppliers, and high labor costs.

Problem-Solving Techniques for Engineering Challenges

When it comes to existing products or processes, before we can assign an engineering team to create solutions for the challenges you face, we must first ensure that they understand your processes and why your team made the engineering choices it did.

Root Cause Analysis

We use multiple techniques to delve into the issues that are preventing system optimization. Some of the approaches we employ include:

  • 5 Whys: In this method, the team analyzes the system five times, with each discrete analysis finding new symptoms and problems.
  • Ishikawa Diagram: Also referred to as a “fishbone diagram.” It divides potential causes into categories such as materials, machinery, staffing, methods, and facilities.
  • Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA): This approach focuses on detecting multiple points of failure.

Lean Manufacturing Principles

The aim here is to minimize waste and maximize potential. Our engineers investigate the manufacturing processes that are causing some products to lose value.

Integrating Advanced Technologies

One area of inefficiency we frequently discover when we partner with manufacturing operations is the lack of integration of advanced technologies. Here are some examples:

  • Automation: Our team can review your plastic molding processes to determine whether automation is a viable solution.
  • Artificial Intelligence: AI can monitor equipment to determine whether it is working at optimal levels and anticipate malfunctions.
  • Internet of Things: Embedded sensors can collect data on inventory levels and equipment performance.
  • Digital Twins: A digital twin is an identical digital rendering of a machine or system that can identify issues within your system.
